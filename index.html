Umrah is the minor pilgrimage to Makkah that can be performed any time of the year. The core rites are entering Ihram, performing Tawaf around the Kaaba, completing Sa'i between Safa and Marwah, and exiting Ihram with hair trimming.

Most travelers require an Umrah visa or eligible eVisa. Requirements typically include a passport valid for at least 6 months, return tickets, confirmed accommodation, and health documentation per Saudi regulations.

A valid passport, recent passport photos, flight and hotel confirmations, and any required vaccination certificates. Additional documents may be needed based on nationality and current entry rules.

Enter Ihram at the appropriate Miqat, perform Tawaf (7 circuits), pray at Maqam Ibrahim, drink Zamzam, complete Sa'i (7 trips) between Safa and Marwah, then trim or shave hair to exit Ihram.

The rites can typically be completed within a day, often in 4–6 hours depending on crowd levels. Trips usually range from 3–7 days to allow for travel, rest, and worship.

Women and children can perform Umrah. Rules around guardianship, consent, and documentation vary; always check current Saudi guidelines and airline policies before travel.
